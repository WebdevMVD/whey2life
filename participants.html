Beneficiaries

Coordinator and Partners

By co-located the bio-refinery with an existing anaerobic digestion facility and by using a second generation feedstock as it principal input, Whey2LIFE will realise greater resource efficiency by implementing this industrial symbiosis.

NaringTech

Addressing the market demand for high quality non-GMO yeast for European animal feed applications and want to avail of first mover advantage utilising dairy waste streams as a cost effective growing media.

Lambers-Segers

LAMBERS-SEGHERS is a concept of independent agriculture enterprising in Belgium. “Free sale” is our goal associated with flexibility, transparency and professionalism.

Ormonde Organics

Ormonde Organics converts organic waste from every sector into power, heat, fuel and soil amendment products using safe, natural bioscience. The company was founded in 1993 and our head office is in Waterford, Ireland with new plants planned all over Ireland.

DCU

The Energy and Design laboratory in DCU was established to facilitate fundamental and applied research in energy engineering, with a particular emphasis on the design, modelling, and analysis of sustainable energy systems and installations and the design of energy efficient devices.

LIFE programme

The LIFE programme is the EU’s funding instrument for the environment and climate action created in 1992. The current funding period 2014-2020 has a budget of €3.4 billion.

Whey2LIFE will extend the whey permeate value chain by developing an integrated biorefinery to provide an alternate and sustainable valorisation route for whey permeate.
